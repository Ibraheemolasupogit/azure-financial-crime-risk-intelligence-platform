# AML Controls Matrix

This matrix documents synthetic analytical controls for portfolio demonstration. It is not a regulatory control inventory, certification, or statement of compliance.

| Rule ID | Typology | Trigger | Data sources | Severity | Evidence produced | Known false-positive scenarios | Investigator review recommendation | Azure implementation mapping |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| AML001 | High-value activity | Amount exceeds configured threshold | Transactions | High | Amount, threshold, type, channel, merchant country | Property, tax, payroll, treasury, or other legitimate large payments | Confirm customer/account context, source and purpose of funds | Event Hubs plus Stream Analytics; ADLS evidence |
| AML002 | Structuring or smurfing | Multiple sub-reporting-threshold transactions exceed rolling count and aggregate limits | Transactions, customers | High | Count, cumulative amount, window, reporting and aggregate thresholds | Regular cash-intensive business activity, split invoices, recurring transfers | Review transaction sequence, counterpart context, and expected activity | Stream Analytics windows; Synapse historical analysis |
| AML003 | Rapid movement of funds | Material activity follows prior activity within a short window with material value or context change | Transactions | High | Prior timestamp, elapsed minutes, amounts, channel and country changes | Legitimate rapid purchases, travel, bill payment, treasury operations | Reconstruct flow and compare with customer profile and destination | Functions for event state; Synapse path analysis |
| AML004 | Geography monitoring | Merchant/IP matches synthetic configured list or customer, merchant, and IP show unexpected mismatch | Transactions, sessions, customers | Medium | Customer, merchant and IP countries, classification, cross-border flag | Travel, VPNs, roaming, multinational commerce | Validate location context without treating geography as proof of wrongdoing | Event Hubs enrichment; Purview classification |
| AML005 | Cross-border behaviour | Rolling cross-border count or ratio exceeds configured customer threshold | Transactions, customers | Medium | Prior count, ratio, countries, current country, window thresholds | Expatriates, travel, international businesses, remittances | Compare with historical behaviour, occupation, and expected corridors | Stream Analytics windows; Synapse aggregates |
| AML006 | Dormant reactivation | Material transaction follows configured inactivity period | Transactions, accounts | High | Inactivity duration, prior date, amount, threshold, account status | Seasonal accounts, savings withdrawals, planned reactivation | Confirm account ownership, purpose, and preceding access events | Functions state store; ADLS and Synapse history |
| AML007 | Repeated failures | Multiple declined or reversed transactions occur within rolling window | Transactions, devices | Medium | Failure count, window, account, channel, device | Insufficient funds, merchant issues, customer input errors | Review authentication, device history, and subsequent successful activity | Stream Analytics count windows; Monitor telemetry |
| AML008 | Device or session risk | Risky session, new device, failed login, or unusual IP is associated with transaction | Transactions, device sessions, customers | Medium | Device/session IDs, authentication, login result, IP, session signal, novelty | New phones, travel, VPNs, shared devices, password errors | Validate device enrolment and authentication sequence | Functions enrichment; Application Insights telemetry |
| AML009 | Due-diligence exposure | Synthetic KYC, PEP, screening, or watchlist indicator requires review | Customers, AML watchlist | High | Synthetic customer ID and due-diligence status indicators only | Pending periodic review, resolved screening similarity, stale watchlist record | Check current due-diligence record and disposition; avoid unnecessary personal details | Purview governance; Synapse customer controls |
| AML010 | Merchant or channel pattern | Configured category/channel or jointly novel merchant-channel behaviour appears | Transactions | Low | Category, channel, prior patterns, configured conditions | New legitimate spending, product adoption, travel, customer lifecycle changes | Compare with prior activity and other alerts before escalation | Event Hubs rules; Power BI scenario reporting |
